# ECONOMICS.md

## How This Tool Makes Money for Credex

The AI Spend Audit tool itself is free.

The real business comes after the audit.

The goal of the tool is to:

- help startups discover overspending
- collect qualified leads
- connect high-saving users with Credex credits

Instead of selling the audit, Credex uses the audit to find companies already spending heavily on AI tools.

---

## What a Converted Lead Could Be Worth

A startup using multiple AI tools can easily spend:

- $500/month
- $2,000/month
- sometimes much more

For this estimate, I will assume the average paying customer spends around:

$2,000/month on AI tools and APIs.

If Credex earns around 10% margin from selling discounted AI credits:

- Monthly revenue per customer:
  $2,000 × 10%
  = $200/month

- Annual revenue per customer:
  $200 × 12
  = $2,400/year

This means one converted company could realistically be worth around $2,400 per year.

---

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

The product has strong organic distribution potential because:

- founders discuss AI costs publicly
- audit reports are shareable
- savings numbers create curiosity
- users naturally forward reports internally

The first users would likely come from:

- Hacker News
- Indie Hackers
- X/Twitter
- Reddit communities
- founder Slack/Discord groups

Because of this, the early acquisition cost could stay relatively low.

Estimated early-stage CAC:

- $20–$100 per paying customer

This is possible because the product depends more on:

- community distribution
- screenshots
- referrals
- discussions

than paid ads.

## Example Conversion Funnel

Example monthly funnel:

10,000 visitors
↓
3,000 users complete an audit
↓
900 users share email
↓
90 users book consultation calls
↓
18 companies purchase credits

This assumes:

- 30% audit completion rate
- 30% email capture rate
- 10% consultation booking rate
- 20% consultation-to-purchase conversion

These numbers are rough estimates, but they are realistic for a focused B2B tool solving a real cost problem.

---

## Revenue Example

If 18 companies convert monthly:

18 × $2,400 annual value
= $43,200 ARR added per month

Over time, recurring customers could compound into meaningful annual revenue.

---

## Why the Economics Work

This product works financially because:

- audits are cheap to generate
- AI summaries cost very little
- most traffic can come organically
- the value of one converted customer is high

The audit acts like a lead-generation engine instead of a standalone product.

The cost to serve one more audit is small, but even a few conversions can create strong revenue.

---

## What Would Need to Be True to Reach $1M ARR

If one customer is worth around:
$2,400/year

Then Credex would need around:

$1,000,000 ÷ $2,400
≈ 417 active customers

To reach this scale, the product would need:

- consistent organic traffic
- strong sharing behavior
- good consultation conversion rates
- trust in the audit recommendations

The public audit URLs and social sharing features are important because they help reduce customer acquisition costs over time.

---

## Biggest Risk

The biggest risk is that users may complete the audit but never book a consultation.

Many startups may:

- want free recommendations
- optimize internally
- avoid sales calls

Because of this, the audit experience must build trust by:

- giving honest recommendations
- avoiding fake savings
- clearly explaining reasoning
- showing transparent calculations

---

## Why This Could Still Become Valuable

AI tooling costs are increasing quickly, especially for engineering-heavy startups.

Many teams:

- buy overlapping subscriptions
- overpay for enterprise plans
- never compare alternatives properly

As AI adoption grows, companies will likely care more about:

- cost visibility
- optimization
- benchmarking

That creates a real opportunity for a product focused specifically on AI spend optimization instead of general SaaS expense tracking.
