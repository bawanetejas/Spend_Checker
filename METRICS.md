# METRICS.md

## North Star Metric

The main metric I would track is:

Completed audits per week

I chose this because the product only creates value when users finish the audit and see their savings report. If users do not complete the audit, there are no leads, no report sharing, and no consultation opportunities for Credex.

This product is not something people use daily, so tracking weekly completed audits makes more sense than daily active users.

---

## Input Metrics

### 1. Audit Start Rate

This tracks how many visitors actually begin the audit after landing on the website.

This helps measure:

- landing page clarity
- CTA strength
- user interest

If users visit the site but do not start the audit, the messaging or homepage likely needs improvement.

---

### 2. Audit Completion Rate

This tracks how many users finish the audit after starting it.

This helps identify:

- confusing UX
- too many form fields
- slow loading issues
- trust problems

If many users leave before finishing, I would simplify the flow and reduce friction.

---

### 3. Report Share Rate

This tracks how many users share their public audit result.

I think this is important because the product has strong viral potential. If users share reports, it likely means:

- the results feel valuable
- savings are meaningful
- users trust the recommendations

Low sharing may mean the report is not interesting or useful enough.

---

## Other Important Metrics

I would also track:

- email capture rate
- consultation booking rate

These metrics are important because the tool mainly works as a lead-generation product for Credex.

---

## What I Would Track First

The first analytics events I would track are:

- landing page visits
- audit started
- audit completed
- email submitted
- report shared
- consultation booked

These events would help identify where users drop off and which parts of the product create the most engagement.

---

## What Would Make Me Change Direction

One major warning sign would be a very low audit completion rate.

For example, if less than 20% of users complete the audit, it likely means:

- the process feels too long
- the value is unclear
- users do not trust the product enough

Another warning sign would be users completing audits but never sharing reports, because the product depends heavily on organic sharing and referrals.
