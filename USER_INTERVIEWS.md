## User Interview — Nikhil (AI Engineer)

### Q1. How do you currently track your team's spend on AI tools?

**Response:**  
AI tool spending is currently tracked manually without a dedicated management system.

---

### Q2. What is the most frustrating part about managing these subscriptions?

**Response:**  
The biggest challenge is overspending caused by forgetting to pause or manage subscriptions on time.

---

### Q3. If a tool showed you that you could save $200/month by switching plans, would that be enough to make you actually switch?

**Response:**  
Nikhil mentioned that cost savings alone would not be enough to switch. However, he expressed strong interest in a platform that could centrally track all active AI subscriptions and provide visibility into spending.

---

### Key Insight / Surprising Observation

Nikhil highlighted that he has not seen any existing tool that allows users to input their subscription plans and receive insights into whether they are overspending. He showed genuine interest in the idea and requested access to the product once it is available.

---

### Design Feedback

- The current design direction is acceptable.
- Building user trust and maintaining transparency should remain the top priority.

## User Interview 2 — Ravinder (Team Lead)

### Q1. How do you currently track your team's spend on AI tools?

**Response:**

- Currently, there is no dedicated system for tracking AI tool spending.
- Different AI models are used depending on the specific use case.
- Spending is indirectly controlled by limiting the team to a small number of subscriptions.

---

### Q2. What is the most frustrating part about managing these subscriptions?

**Response:**

- Lack of clarity about which AI tool performs best for a specific use case.
- No centralized dashboard to monitor subscriptions and usage.
- Difficulty identifying the most cost-effective tool for particular tasks.
- No visibility into token wastage or inefficient usage patterns.

---

### Q3. If a tool showed you that you could save $200/month by switching plans, would that be enough to make you actually switch?

**Response:**  
Ravinder mentioned that he would definitely consider switching tools or plans if the recommendations were honest, trustworthy, and did not negatively impact the team’s productivity or workflow.

---

### Key Insight / Surprising Observation

Ravinder expressed strong interest in discovering alternative tools that provide similar functionality at a lower cost. He believes this would significantly help teams reduce unnecessary AI spending while maintaining productivity.

---

### Design Feedback

- The current design direction is acceptable and does not require major changes.
- However, user trust should be treated as the highest priority throughout the product experience.
- Transparency, honest recommendations, and credibility are critical for adoption.

## User Interview — Priya (Engineering Manager)

### Q1. How do you currently track your team's spend on AI tools?

**Response:**  
AI spending is primarily tracked through invoices and shared spreadsheets. Since different teams use different AI tools, managing everything in one place becomes difficult.

---

### Q2. What is the most frustrating part about managing these subscriptions?

**Response:**

- Lack of visibility into which subscriptions are actively being used
- Multiple teams paying for similar or overlapping tools
- Difficulty tracking total monthly AI spending
- Challenges explaining increasing AI costs during budget or review discussions

---

### Q3. If a tool showed you that you could save $200/month by switching plans, would that be enough to make you actually switch?

**Response:**  
Priya mentioned that she would consider switching plans if the recommendations were reliable and did not negatively affect team productivity or workflow efficiency.

---

### Key Insight / Surprising Observation

Priya highlighted that AI subscription management becomes increasingly difficult as teams scale. She felt that a simple dashboard showing subscriptions, usage patterns, and potential savings opportunities would provide strong practical value for engineering teams.

---

### Design Feedback

- Keep the interface simple and easy to trust
- Clearly explain why recommendations are being made
- Prioritize transparency over excessive features
